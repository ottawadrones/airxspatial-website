# Product

brand

## Users

AirXSpatial serves three audiences, in this priority order for the current
(pre-portal, credibility) phase:

1. **Customers** — real-estate developers, general contractors, and
   infrastructure & asset owners who need surveying, inspection, LiDAR, or
   photogrammetry done on real-world sites. Their context: a high-stakes
   project where compliance, maintenance, or construction-progress tracking
   depends on accurate spatial data, and they need to trust the work is done
   to standard. They arrive evaluating whether AirXSpatial is credible enough
   to handle work that matters.
2. **Supply — engineering firms & certified pilots** who want a reliable
   pipeline of qualified work to bid on and win. Their context: they have
   capability and capacity, and want demand routed to them without doing their
   own sales and marketing.
3. **Investors / partners** evaluating the company and the category bet — the
   "marketplace for engineering & survey" thesis.

## Product Purpose

AirXSpatial is a two-sided marketplace — the "Amazon of engineering &
survey." Customers publish RFPs for surveying, inspection, LiDAR, and
photogrammetry of real-world sites; engineering firms and certified drone/
aircraft pilots bid on and deliver the work. AirXSpatial is the
sales-and-marketing layer that makes it easy for supply to win work and easy
for customers to get rigorous spatial deliverables.

The bidding portal itself comes later. **Right now the site's job is
credibility**: make the world's largest developers feel that the most serious,
precise people in the industry are behind this — so they want to work with
AirXSpatial. Success is the visitor leaving convinced this is institutional-
grade, not a startup experiment.

## Brand Personality

**Monumental · precise · trustworthy.** The site should feel like
award-winning architecture — the Burj Khalifa, One World Trade Center.
Glass reflecting sky, structure, landscape; quiet confidence and engineered
precision; a deliberate "ooh and aah." Voice is calm, exact, and senior —
never loud, playful, or hype-driven. It speaks the way a top-tier engineering
or architecture firm speaks: understated authority, proof over promises.

## Anti-references

- Neon / glow startup aesthetics (the look being removed from this repo).
- Generic SaaS gradient-purple landing pages; "modern and clean" template feel.
- Gig-economy / Uber-casual marketplace energy. This is institutional and
  premium, not a side-hustle app.
- Anything cheap, busy, clip-arty, emoji-driven, or "techy for techy's sake."
- Overclaiming, growth-hacky urgency, or stock-photo-of-people-shaking-hands
  corporate filler.

## Design Principles

1. **Monumental restraint.** Vast negative space, few elements, each one
   deliberate. The luxury is in what is left out. One idea per view.
2. **Material honesty.** Glass, structure, sky, landscape. Real imagery of
   real sites carries the emotion; the UI gets out of the way and frames it.
3. **Engineered precision.** Exact alignment, a strict grid, tabular figures
   where data appears. The craft of the page signals the rigor of the work.
4. **Earn the "ooh."** One or two moments of genuine awe per page (a hero, a
   reveal) surrounded by calm. Drama is rationed, not constant.
5. **Trust over hype.** Show standards, proof, and credibility — certifications,
   real deliverables, real sites. Never overpromise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, non-text UI ≥3:1.
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support — every reveal/parallax has a
  crossfade or static fallback.
- Color is never the sole carrier of meaning (status, links, data all carry a
  second cue).
- Imagery-forward design must remain legible: text over photography always
  sits on a scrim or solid panel meeting contrast.
